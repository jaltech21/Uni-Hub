## Department Reports

Department reports provide comprehensive insights into your department's activity, user engagement, and content statistics.

### Report Types

#### 1. Basic Overview Report
The Basic Overview provides essential department metrics at a glance.

**What's Included:**
- Total number of users in your department
- Number of active vs. inactive users
- Total content created (assignments, quizzes, notes)
- Recent activity count
- User engagement percentage

**How to Generate:**
1. Go to your department's Reports page
2. Find the "Basic Overview" section
3. Click the "Generate Basic Report" button
4. Wait for the report to load (usually 5-10 seconds)

**Understanding the Metrics:**
- **Total Users**: All members currently in your department
- **Active Users**: Users who have logged in within the last 30 days
- **Total Content**: Sum of all assignments, quizzes, notes, and announcements
- **Recent Activity**: Actions performed in the last 7 days
- **Engagement Rate**: Percentage of users who are actively participating

#### 2. Detailed Analysis Report
The Detailed Analysis provides in-depth statistics and breakdowns.

**What's Included:**
- Individual user statistics and activity levels
- Content breakdown by type and creator
- Activity timeline showing trends over time
- Top performers and most active users

**How to Generate:**
1. Navigate to the "Detailed Analysis" section
2. Click "Generate Detailed Report"
3. The report may take 15-30 seconds to generate

**Key Sections:**
- **User Statistics**: Individual performance metrics for each department member
- **Content Breakdown**: Analysis of different content types and their popularity
- **Activity Timeline**: Visual representation of department activity over time

#### 3. Executive Summary Report
The Executive Summary provides high-level insights and recommendations.

**What's Included:**
- Overall department performance summary
- Key insights and trends
- Actionable recommendations
- Performance comparisons

**How to Generate:**
1. Scroll to the "Executive Summary" section
2. Click "Generate Summary Report"
3. Review the generated insights and recommendations

### Using Date Filters

All reports can be filtered by date range to focus on specific time periods.

**Setting Custom Date Range:**
1. Look for the date filter controls at the top of the reports page
2. Click on "Start Date" and select your desired start date
3. Click on "End Date" and select your desired end date
4. All generated reports will use this date range

**Quick Date Presets:**
- **Last 7 Days**: Shows activity from the past week
- **Last 30 Days**: Shows activity from the past month
- **Last 3 Months**: Shows activity from the past quarter
- **Custom Range**: Allows you to set specific start and end dates

### Interpreting Report Data

#### Understanding User Engagement
- **High Engagement (80%+)**: Department is very active with strong participation
- **Moderate Engagement (50-79%)**: Good activity levels with room for improvement
- **Low Engagement (<50%)**: May need initiatives to increase participation

#### Content Creation Trends
- **Increasing Trend**: More content being created over time (positive)
- **Stable Trend**: Consistent content creation (good maintenance)
- **Decreasing Trend**: May need attention to encourage more content creation

## Export Functions

Export your reports in multiple formats for sharing, archiving, or further analysis.

### Available Export Formats

#### 1. CSV (Comma-Separated Values)
**Best for:**
- Importing into Excel or Google Sheets
- Data analysis and manipulation
- Creating custom charts and graphs

**What's Included:**
- All report data in spreadsheet format
- Headers for each data column
- Raw numerical data

#### 2. PDF (Portable Document Format)
**Best for:**
- Professional presentations
- Printing and sharing
- Archival purposes

**What's Included:**
- Formatted report with charts and graphs
- Professional layout and styling
- Summary tables and key insights

#### 3. Excel (XLSX)
**Best for:**
- Advanced Excel analysis
- Creating pivot tables
- Sharing with users who prefer Excel

**What's Included:**
- Multiple worksheets for different data types
- Formatted tables and basic charts
- Formulas for calculated fields

### How to Export Reports

#### Exporting from Reports Page
1. Navigate to the Department Reports page
2. Generate the report you want to export (Basic, Detailed, or Summary)
3. Look for the "Export Options" section
4. Click on your preferred format:
   - **CSV Export**: Downloads a .csv file
   - **PDF Export**: Downloads a .pdf file  
   - **Excel Export**: Downloads a .xlsx file

#### Export File Naming
Files are automatically named with the following pattern:
- `department_[report_type]_report_[date].extension`
- Example: `department_basic_report_2024-01-15.pdf`

#### Export Processing Time
- **CSV**: Usually instant (less than 5 seconds)
- **PDF**: May take 10-30 seconds depending on data size
- **Excel**: May take 15-45 seconds for complex reports

### Working with Exported Data

#### CSV Files
1. Open with Excel, Google Sheets, or any spreadsheet application
2. Data is in raw format - perfect for creating custom analyses
3. Use filters and sorting to explore the data
4. Create charts and pivot tables as needed

#### PDF Files
1. Open with any PDF reader (Adobe Reader, browser, etc.)
2. Use for presentations or printing
3. Contains pre-formatted charts and summaries
4. Professional appearance suitable for sharing with stakeholders

#### Excel Files
1. Open with Microsoft Excel or compatible applications
2. Multiple worksheets organize different types of data
3. Built-in formatting and basic charts included
4. Perfect for users familiar with Excel

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues and Solutions

#### "Access Denied" or "Forbidden" Errors
**Problem**: You see an error message saying you don't have permission to access reports or activity feed.

**Solutions:**
1. Ensure you're logged into your account
2. Verify you're a member of the department you're trying to access
3. Check that your account role has the necessary permissions
4. Contact your department administrator if the problem persists

#### Reports Not Loading or Showing "No Data"
**Problem**: Reports appear empty or fail to generate.

**Possible Causes & Solutions:**
1. **Date Range Too Narrow**: Expand your date range to include more data
2. **New Department**: If your department is new, there may not be enough data yet
3. **Internet Connection**: Check your internet connection and try refreshing
4. **Browser Issues**: Clear your browser cache or try a different browser

#### Activity Feed Not Updating
**Problem**: New activities aren't appearing in the feed.

**Solutions:**
1. Refresh the page to see the latest activities
2. Clear any filters that might be hiding new activities
3. Check if you're viewing an old date range
4. Ensure your internet connection is stable

#### Export Downloads Failing
**Problem**: Export files won't download or appear corrupted.

**Solutions:**
1. Check that popup blockers aren't preventing downloads
2. Ensure you have enough disk space for the file
3. Try a different export format if one isn't working
4. Wait for any currently running exports to complete before starting a new one

#### Performance Issues
**Problem**: Pages load slowly or time out.

**Solutions:**
1. Try accessing during off-peak hours
2. Reduce the date range for large reports
3. Clear your browser cache and cookies
4. Close other browser tabs to free up memory

### Browser Compatibility

**Recommended Browsers:**
- Chrome (latest version)
- Firefox (latest version)
- Safari (latest version)
- Edge (latest version)

**Known Issues:**
- Internet Explorer is not supported
- Very old browser versions may have display issues
- Mobile browsers work but desktop is recommended for full functionality

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: How often is the data updated?**
A: Activity feed data updates in real-time. Report data is refreshed every 5-15 minutes depending on the type of report.

**Q: Can I schedule automatic report generation?**
A: Currently, reports must be generated manually. Scheduled reporting may be added in future updates.

**Q: Who can see my department's reports?**
A: Only members of your department can view its reports and activity feed. Department administrators have access to all features, while regular members may have limited access to certain detailed information.

### Reports Questions

**Q: What's the difference between the three report types?**
A: 
- **Basic**: Quick overview with key metrics
- **Detailed**: Comprehensive analysis with breakdowns and timelines
- **Summary**: High-level insights and recommendations for decision-makers

**Q: How far back does historical data go?**
A: Report data goes back to when your department was created in the system. However, very old data (2+ years) may be archived and not included in standard reports.

**Q: Can I compare my department to others?**
A: Currently, reports only show your own department's data. Cross-department comparisons may be available to university administrators.

### Activity Feed Questions

**Q: Why don't I see all activities in the feed?**
A: The activity feed shows the most relevant activities. Some system-generated activities or very minor actions may be filtered out to keep the feed useful.

**Q: Can I get notifications for specific activities?**
A: The current system doesn't include notification features, but this is planned for future releases.

**Q: How long are activities stored?**
A: Activities are stored indefinitely, but very old activities (1+ year) may be archived and only accessible through special requests.

### Export Questions

**Q: Why is my PDF export taking so long?**
A: PDF generation is more complex than CSV exports, especially for detailed reports with charts. Large amounts of data can take 30+ seconds to process.

**Q: Can I customize the export format?**
A: The current exports use standard formats. Custom formatting options may be added in future updates.

**Q: Are there limits on how many reports I can export?**
A: Yes, there are rate limits to prevent system overload:
- Maximum 10 exports per hour per user
- Large reports may count as multiple exports

### Technical Questions

**Q: My browser is blocking the download. What should I do?**
A: 
1. Check if your browser has a popup blocker that's preventing downloads
2. Look for a download notification in your browser's download bar
3. Try right-clicking the export button and selecting "Save link as..."

**Q: The exported Excel file won't open properly. Why?**
A: 
1. Ensure you have a compatible application (Excel, LibreOffice, Google Sheets)
2. Try downloading again - the file may have been corrupted during download
3. Check that your antivirus software isn't quarantining the file

**Q: Can I access these features on my mobile device?**
A: Yes, but the experience is optimized for desktop use. Some features like complex report generation and exports work better on desktop computers.

- Use appropriate date ranges - too broad may slow performance, too narrow may not show trends
- Generate detailed reports during off-peak hours for better performance
- Save important reports as exports for future reference
- Review reports regularly to track department progress

### For Activity Feed
- Use filters to focus on activities relevant to your role
- Check the feed regularly to stay updated on department activities
- Use the "Load More" feature to see historical context
- Pay attention to activity patterns to understand department dynamics
